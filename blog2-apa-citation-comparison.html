Most Common Mistakes Indian Students Make

‚ùå
Using APA when Harvard is required

They look nearly identical but differ on ampersand vs "and," title case, and publisher location. Always check your module handbook first.

‚ùå
Forgetting the access date for websites

Harvard and Chicago require an "Accessed" date for webpages. APA does not ‚Äî but students apply one rule incorrectly across all styles.

‚ùå
Wrong title capitalisation in APA

APA uses sentence case (first word only). Students apply title case ‚Äî Every Word Capitalised ‚Äî which is correct in MLA and Chicago but wrong in APA.

‚ùå
Missing page numbers in MLA

MLA requires a page number for both quotes and paraphrases. APA only requires it for direct quotes.

‚ùå
Formatting DOI as plain text

APA 7th requires DOIs as full hyperlinks (https://doi.org/...). Many students paste only the raw DOI number without the prefix.

‚ùå
Mixing styles in one document

APA in-text citations with a Harvard-formatted reference list ‚Äî or vice versa ‚Äî is immediately visible to markers and costs significant marks.

‚ö†Ô∏è Harvard Warning for Indian Students

  • There is no single official "Harvard" standard ‚Äî it is a family of author-date styles.
  • Your university's library or handbook specifies their version ‚Äî follow that exactly, not generic online guides.
  • Tools like Cite This For Me or Zotero may generate a version of Harvard that does not match your institution's requirements.

Zotero is free, integrates with Word and Google Docs, and supports all four major citation styles ‚Äî set it up on Day 1.

Which Referencing Tool Should You Use?

Zotero is free, integrates directly with Word and Google Docs, and supports all four major styles. Mendeley is a strong alternative for APA-heavy disciplines. Web tools like Cite This For Me are convenient but should always be verified manually ‚Äî automated generators produce errors, especially with unusual sources like government reports, edited volumes, or conference papers.

The golden rule: verify every reference entry manually against your institution's guide before you submit. The responsibility for correct referencing always belongs to the student, not the software.
