Browser Lockdown: Setup That Actually Works

A misconfigured system the morning of your exam is the biggest avoidable problem. Here is the setup timeline that eliminates technical issues before they happen.

7d
One week before: Install and test the lockdown browser

Download from your university's IT page ‚Äî not a third-party source. Run the practice test your institution provides. If it fails, you have time to contact IT support.

1d
One day before: Full environment check

Run the webcam and microphone test built into the platform. Check internet speed (minimum 5 Mbps up/download). Clear your desk. Charge laptop to 100% and keep the charger plugged in.

1h
One hour before: Final preparations

Close all background applications. Restart your laptop fresh. Log into the exam platform and complete identity verification. Position your webcam so your face, shoulders, and both hands are visible.

During the exam: If something goes wrong

Do not panic and do not close the exam window. Use the live chat or helpline (save the number before you begin). Document the issue ‚Äî time, what happened, screenshot if possible.

Time Management in a 3-Hour Proctored Exam

With no room cues and no sight of other students working, pacing is entirely on you. A written time plan before you start is non-negotiable.

First 10 minutes

Minutes 10‚Äì60

What Invigilators Are Actually Looking For

‚úÖ What Invigilators Accept as Normal

  • Brief glances to the side while thinking ‚Äî this is universal and expected
  • Moving your lips when reading complex problems
  • Drinking water or briefly looking away from the screen
  • Adjusting your sitting position or leaning closer to the screen
  • Brief pauses between typing ‚Äî thinking time is normal

üö´ What Genuinely Triggers Serious Review

  • Sustained off-screen eye movement in a consistent direction (suggesting off-screen reading)
  • Another voice clearly audible giving answers or reading questions aloud
  • A second person visible in the frame or their shadow on the wall
  • Disappearing from the webcam frame entirely for more than a few seconds
  • Earphones or wireless earbuds visible anywhere in the frame
  • Phone or second screen visible anywhere in the camera's field of view

Mental Performance on Exam Day

Students who score 80+ consistently share one habit: they do not start revising the morning of the exam. The morning of a proctored exam should be used for setup, a calm breakfast, a short walk, and reviewing your time plan ‚Äî not for new information that creates anxiety without benefit.

Sleep the night before is not optional. A well-rested brain with solid preparation outperforms an exhausted brain that revised until 3am every single time. Treat the night before your exam as part of the exam itself ‚Äî because it is.

  • Identity verification, room scanning, and system checks typically take 15‚Äì20 minutes before the exam begins.
  • Log on at least 30 minutes before your scheduled start time ‚Äî not 5 minutes before.
  • If your exam is at 10:00am, your laptop should be open and on the platform by 9:30am at the latest.

Online proctored exams reward preparation, structure, and composure ‚Äî three things entirely within your control. The technology is watching for clear violations, not for the normal behaviours of a student working hard under pressure.
