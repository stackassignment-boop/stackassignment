1. Analysis of the correct policy

Policy name: National Strategic Framework for Chronic Conditions

Purpose and objectives: The National Strategic Framework for Chronic Conditions (the Framework) is Australia's framework policy guiding actions to reduce the impacts of chronic conditions and improve the health outcomes of Australians. The Framework's main aim is to ensure collaborative, person-centered, and sustainable chronic care. The Framework establishes three interrelated objectives:

  1. Prevention for all Australians,
  2. Efficient, effective, and appropriate care for individuals living with chronic conditions, and
  3. Priority populations at risk of experiencing poor health outcomes.

These objectives are oriented toward addressing the increasing burden of chronic disease in Australia, since chronic conditions are the largest contributing factor to illness, disability, and death. The policy seeks to align the actions of governments, health professionals, and communities to deliver better-integrated care that emphasizes prevention, health literacy, and self-management.
